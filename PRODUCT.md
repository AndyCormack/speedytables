# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Two audiences, weighted equally. Frontend and Svelte developers evaluating whether SpeedyTables is a credible high-performance alternative to AG Grid — they read benchmark numbers, poke at scenario pages, and judge both the milliseconds and the craft. And the maintainers themselves, who live in these pages daily as a benchmarking workbench. The same surface must serve both: an internal instrument that is presentable as public evidence at any moment.

## Product Purpose

A demo and benchmark application for SpeedyTables, a headless Svelte data grid built to stay fast at a million rows. Every scenario page is simultaneously a working example and a recorded benchmark against AG Grid on identical data. Success: a visitor leaves convinced the performance claims are real, and the maintainers can measure any feature in one command.

## Positioning

The grid that proves it: every feature ships with recorded head-to-head numbers at a million rows.

## Brand Personality

Clean, calm, modern — but tighter than the airy SaaS default: modest whitespace, minimal rounding, nothing extreme. Pleasant and crisp, refined and engineered, with smooth, deliberate polish. An instrument, not a brochure.

## Anti-references

- SaaS-template slop: gradient heroes, glassmorphism, identical card grids, dashboard-template chrome.
- Not a clone of AG Grid's Quartz theme — comparable polish, its own identity.
- Dated enterprise data-tool gray-on-gray (classic ExtJS/jqGrid energy).

## Design Principles

- **The numbers are the interface.** Benchmark results and grid data lead; decoration never competes with evidence.
- **Performance is visible.** Nothing in the UI may cost milliseconds the benchmarks would expose; motion is cheap, purposeful, and honest.
- **Instrument, not brochure.** Density and legibility over spectacle; every control earns its pixels.
- **Comparable polish, own identity.** Stand next to AG Grid Quartz without imitating it.
- **Accessible by default.** The headless components ship keyboard-operable and screen-reader-sane out of the box.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast, full keyboard operability (sorting, filter inputs, dropdowns), visible focus indicators, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored everywhere.
